The Fountains Manifesto

The Science of
AI Orchestration

AI is a powerful instrument, but you are the conductor. These are the 7 core principles discovered at Fountains Data Technology for building high-end software with artificial intelligence.

01

The Architectural Blueprint

Before touching an AI tool, you must have a crystal-clear specification of your end goal. AI thrives on context but fails on ambiguity. Define your features, user flow, and visual style first.

Pro Tip: Write a "System Prompt" document that describes your project as if you were explaining it to a human junior developer.
02

Code Literacy & Command

You cannot lead what you do not understand. Deepen your knowledge in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This allows you to speak "technical language" to the AI, ensuring the code it generates is efficient and clean.

Pro Tip: When the AI gives you code, read every line. If you don't understand a function, ask the AI to explain it. Never copy-paste blindly.
03

The Multi-Model Ecosystem

Don't be loyal to a single tool. Claude might be better at high-end UI/CSS, while GPT-4o might excel at complex Logic, and Perplexity for real-time API research. Use the right tool for the right task.

Pro Tip: If one AI is stuck in a loop, move the code to another AI. A "fresh pair of digital eyes" often solves the bug instantly.
04

Iterative Resilience

AI can be frustrating, hallucinate, or give broken code. Persistence is the key. Development with AI is not a "one-click" miracle; it is a conversation. Stay calm and refine the output.

Pro Tip: When the AI fails, don't just say "it's broken." Copy the error message from your console and feed it back to the AI.
05

Contextual Precision

Words are your code. Using precise expressions like "State Management," "Responsive Flexbox," or "Asynchronous Fetch" directs the AI to use modern industry standards rather than outdated methods.

Pro Tip: Use "Negative Prompting." Tell the AI what NOT to do (e.g., "Do not use external libraries like Bootstrap; use raw CSS Grid").
06

Atomic Development

Never ask an AI to build a "whole website" in one prompt. Break your project into tiny "atoms" (buttons, navbars, cards). Build them perfectly one by one, then assemble the molecule.

Pro Tip: This prevents the AI from hitting "token limits" and ensures the highest code quality for each component.
07

The Verification Protocol

Always maintain the "Human-in-the-loop" status. AI can write code that looks correct but is insecure or slow. You are the final judge of quality, security, and performance.

Pro Tip: Test your site on mobile and across different browsers manually. AI cannot "feel" the user experience yet.
